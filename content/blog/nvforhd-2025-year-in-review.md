## 2024: How It Started

The 2024 inaugural tournament at Old Greenwood Golf Club in Truckee was built from scratch. No established donor base. No track record. Just a mission, a golf course, and a community that showed up.

It sold out.

$25,000 was raised and transferred in full to HelpCureHD. That money went to Brandon and Rylee Puccini — a young Northern Nevada couple carrying the HD gene who could not afford IVF. Rylee is now pregnant with an HD-free baby.

## 2025: Shifting Focus to the Families Already in Need

In 2025, NVforHD made a deliberate choice: to shift focus from preventing future HD (IVF) to supporting the families already living with the disease.

The beneficiary: **UC Davis Huntington's Disease Center of Excellence, Sacramento CA.**

The rationale: Over 90 Northern Nevada families are currently living with HD. They make a 130-mile drive to Sacramento for specialist care because there is no equivalent clinic in Nevada. That clinic operates on private donation. It needed us.

**What the 2025 proceeds funded:**

The UC Davis HD Center team includes 2 neurologists, 2 psychiatrists, 1 genetic counselor, 1 social worker, 2 physical therapists, and 3 research coordinators — all dedicated to HD. NVforHD's contribution went directly to operational funding — keeping this team staffed and accessible.

:::fact UC Davis is one of only 48 HDSA Centers of Excellence nationwide. For Northern Nevada's HD families, it is the only specialist option within reach. Source: HDSA
